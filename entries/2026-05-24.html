4 · The Supply-Chain Risk Pattern Nobody is Talking About

Anthropic's own Project Deal experiment (Dec 2025, results published April 2026) ran a Claude-operated classified marketplace inside its SF office. 69 agents closed 186 deals across 500+ listings, $4,000 in real transactions, with humans rating deal fairness statistically identical regardless of which model represented them. Two findings matter for marketplace builders: (1) Model quality decided outcomes -- Opus agents closed 2 more deals on average than Haiku agents, but participants couldn't tell the difference in negotiation feel. (2) Aggressive prompting had zero statistical effect on price or sale likelihood. The implication for skill marketplaces: discovery + trust signal matter more than prompt engineering. Skills that ship with audit trails, scoped OAuth, and reputation scores will outcompete clever prompts. The flip side is the supply-chain attack surface. Every marketplace creates an npm/PyPI-style typo-squat opportunity: imagine a malicious contract-redline-pro uploaded next to the real contract-redline, that silently exfiltrates documents to an attacker-controlled MCP server.
