What is the Agent Economy 2.0?

The Agent Economy 1.0 was about AI doing tasks -- summarising emails, writing code, answering questions. The Agent Economy 2.0 is about AI spending money. In 2026, agents are becoming first-class economic participants: they hold wallets, initiate payments, buy APIs, and transact with other agents -- autonomously, at sub-second speed, for fractions of a cent. Three foundational layers make this possible: payment protocols (MPP, SPTs), wallet standards (MoonPay Open Wallet), and network rails (Visa, Mastercard, Stripe, Circle). Machine Payments Protocol (MPP) -- Now in Production Launched March 18, 2026 by Stripe + Tempo, MPP is the open standard that allows AI agents to coordinate and execute payments programmatically -- with no human decision point between resource request and payment execution. It's essentially the HTTP of agent payments. Shared Payment Tokens (SPTs) -- How Agents Pay Without

SPTs solve a critical trust problem: how can an agent pay on your behalf without ever seeing your actual card number or bank credentials? Visa Agentic Ready Programme -- Banking Goes Agent-Native Launched March 17, 2026, Visa's Agentic Ready Programme gives banks a structured validation pathway for accepting agent-initiated transactions. This is the 'compliance stamp' that lets conservative financial institutions say yes to agentic payments without rewriting their risk frameworks. Key partners include: Barclays, HSBC UK, Nationwide, Revolut, Banco Santander, Commerzbank, Erste Bank, Raiffeisen Bank International, Nexi Group, and 10+ more across Europe, Middle East, and Latin America.
