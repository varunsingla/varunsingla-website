1. Run an 'automation audit' on your own role

List the top 10 tasks you do weekly. Which are pattern-matching (automatable)? Which require judgment/empathy/creativity? The ratio tells you your urgency level.

3. Study the EU AI Act Annex III list

Know which AI applications are classified 'high-risk' (hiring, credit, education, safety). If your company touches any of these, the Aug 2, 2026 enforcement deadline is non-negotiable.

4. Build a 'golden dataset' for your domain

200-500 input/output pairs where you know the right answer. This is the raw material for evaluating any AI that enters your workflow -- and your leverage over vendors.

5. Follow the Agent SRE role

Even if it's not your job title yet, adopting an Agent SRE mindset -- SLOs on task quality, cost/task, and agent health -- will differentiate you as the person who makes AI actually work in production.
